Iterative Design

With this next project, I explored the value of the design process, emphasizing the importance of critiques. Throughout this project, I had to present my designs to a group critic as well submit my design to an online platform where real designers in the UI industry could offer criticism and show us their thought patterns when interacting with our site so that we could modify it. As with the other previous projects, this project taught me to streamline all aspects of a site. The company we created a mock site for was Vendoo, a business focused on providing multitudes of information from various sites that sellers sell on. The main challenge here was finding how to oragnize the vast array of information potential sellers would receive. As someone focused on efficency and how design most directly correlates with that, I found this project engaging and believe this site could potentially help sellers immensely.

Part 1: Sketching and Wireframing

Sketches

To start the process, every member developed three sketches each in a brainstorming session, designing different possible pages of the start up. Every group member then shared their ideas and received feedback from the group.

Wireframing

After the best elements of each sketch had been chosen by our team, we then proceeded by creating Lo-Fi wireframes that encapsulated these designs. These wireframes allowed us to better visualize on a basic level, the structure of our interface which we were then able to translate into a Hi-Fi prototype.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Part 2: Mockups

Interactive High Fidelity Mockup

Thus, the Hi-Fi prototyping process included adapting our Lo-Fi prototypes into a complete product design. Before we began, we created a style guide to ensure the proper colors, fonts, and icons were chosen.

High-Fidelity Interactive Prototype

Mockup Crit

After developing our high-fidelity prototype, we gathered critiques from fellow classmates on our design and implemented changes based on the feeedback. Here are the critiques:

  • Checkboxes on the Sell page do not have checks
  • Sell page could have preview on the left side
  • Navigation bar and hover effects its very welcoming to new users
  • Position of navigation bar and lines are inconsistent
  • Login and signup are shown to user at once; usually is shown seperately
  • There is no option to go back to sign-in page or to log out
  • About us and signup are on same page
  • Messages page does not display the platform each message is coming from
  • Unread messages on Messages page could be bold
  • Trends page could show analytics for each item
  • Meaning of numbers on trends page are not clear
  • Meaning of up and down arrows on trends page are not clear
  • Product page could filter by item and include a search bar

Part 3: User Testing

The overall task we wanted users to complete was to check the trends of a singular item. We chose this task to see if it was intuitive to click on the image to get a closer picture of the item. The testing instructions and questions were as follows:

After viewing the user testing videos the results of the testing were inline with our expectations. Most of the users when navigating to the trends page intuitively knew that clicking on the image of the shoe displayed a more in depth analysis of the trends. When thinking through their thought process and in the reflection all of the users like the site and felt that it was easy to navigate. One problem arose in our sign in subtask as one user struggled to figure out how to sign in. The user later on realized that they were signing into Figma and not our mockup site. There were no errors or issues on signing out as when they explored the website they all noted that the profile signed them out. Based on our UserTesting results and feedback the only change that we might make is making the sign in button bigger and bolder for the user to see on the home page.
